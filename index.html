Some Basic Protips:

  1. Not Your Keys, Not Your Crypto
    • You should not store your coins at a custodial service unless they are insured and you're getting something out of it (ie. earning interest, more on that later)
    • Do Not purchase your crypto through Paypal or Robinhood (I made this mistake the first time) or other services that do not allow you to withdraw your funds.
    • All of the hacks you hear about in the news are of exchanges/custodial services (and more recently, defi lending programs with smart contract bugs)
    • If you prefer to have your own keys, a cold wallet is preferable. For this I recommend Ledger as they are pretty much the industry standard.

  2. Double, Triple, Quadruple check the address you are sending your crypto to
    • If you send your crypto to the wrong address, it is gone. Transactions are irreversible. This is the nature of cryptocurrencies and the blockchain (it is a feature, not a bug). While this may be scary to some degree, it is not that hard to make sure you are sending it to the correct address.
    • Most wallet addresses are coin-specific (A ledger wallet, for example would have many addresses for each kind of crypto you can contain in it). As a beginner you probably won't need to worry about this, but yes, if you aren't paying attention, you can lose your money.

  5. The 4 Year Cycle
    The Halving is an important event that affects the supply of bitcoin roughly every 4 years (it's actually a factor of how many blocks have been mined which is affected by hashrate and number of transactions, so the exact date can only be predicted up until we are at the event)
    • The 3rd halving occured on May 11, 2020. We are still just at the beginning of this market's bull cycle. The time to buy is now, before the price goes parabolic and enters bubble territory
    • Because Bitcoin is so young and has not gone fully mainstream, many people do not understand the significance of the halving. Until widespread adoption, it follows a bubble --> consolidation --> halving --> bubble market structure as the mainstream chooses to pop its head in now and then to see how things are going

  6. Buy Low/Sell High: Be Patient
    • Based on Bitoin's market cycle, all you need to be profitable is to be smart and be patient.
    • Buy after the market goes through it's bear cycle and reaches a relative bottom. Then sell a couple years after the halving during the next peak. It's that simple.
    • People who don't follow this simple system are throwing their money away and giving Bitcoin a bad name because they don't take the time to do their research and understand how it all works.

  7. Volatility and Dollar-Cost Averaging
    • A lot of people who are risk averse look at the volatility of the crypto market and say it is a reason they will stay out, this is short-sighted. Crypto is growing rapidly.
    • Bitcoin should be considered a long-term investment. The macro view has proven to be relatively predictable based on the halving supply shock, and ever-increasing mainstream adoption.
    • Invest money that you will not need access to for a 1/2/3 years (depending on where you buy in the cycle). Then ignore the short-term pumps/dumps. They ultimately don't matter if you're goal is to ride the market cycle, hopping on and off at appropriate times.
    • As mainstream adoption occurs, bitcoin volatility is ever-decreasing as the markets become more effecient.
    • Volatility works both ways. While people freak out when there is a 30% correction, no one is complaining when it goes up $1k or more in a day. This volatility is what has allowed it to grow so rapidly.
    • The best way to buy bitcoin to mitigate the affects of volatility is to dollar cost average into your position over time. That means buying the dips.
    • Since we are already in the early stages of the bull-run in this cycle, I would recommend dropping a large portion of your intdended investment on the next big dip down to the 20 week moving average, and then making small allocations at subsequent dips, up until we begin to go parabolic.
